About Me

I am a final year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at North Carolina State University. My advisor is Prof. Huaiyu Dai.
My research interests are in the area of complex network analysis. My current research focuses on studying information spreading in multiplex networks and applying network knowledge to the modelling of cell-level dynamics in biological systems.
During my PhD study, I have hands-on experience in analyzing different real social network data mainly from Stanford Large Network Dataset Collection, where I studied multiplex networks formed by different social networks, e.g., Facebook network and Twitter network. I am a follower of data science, I enjoy the process of proposing a hypothesis, running experiment, rejecting hypothesis, and reproposing a new hypothesis. In the end, you will understand your data and use your knowledge to predict the future. This is like training a prophet of the data you are working on, which really excites me. I like to study different kernels in kaggle.
